The Writing Process

Cole and his wife (also a teacher) live in a sunny apartment near Lake Merritt where he hosts students and their parents. He prefers to work in person, especially for the first few sessions, but he also conferences with students through Skype and edits through Google Docs.

THE PERSONAL STATEMENT/COLLEGE ESSAY

The initial meeting is spent discussing the essay prompt(s), the student's goals, and helping the student discover an impressive topic that he or she is passionate about. Every student has countless fantastic essay topics just waiting to be typed out! The key is to match the right topic and tone with the right school. Stanford and Berkeley, for example, are looking for different qualities in an applicant.

Subsequent meetings are spent revising, and eventually polishing, a student's essay until each and every sentence is a perfect combination of aesthetics and functionality.

Cole has coached over a thousand students through the college application process. He continues to be genuinely interested in discovering and displaying a student's passions--and challenges. Colleges want to admit honest and well-rounded students. They are looking for "the real you," not just an invincible version of yourself. Cole will work with you until your personal statement is the best thing you've ever written.

Literary Analysis Essays and Research Papers

Cole works with students (if time allows) from the initial brainstorming stage through the final revision. If time does not allow, Cole recommends the bolded (and most essential) steps.

(Steps 1 and 2 can usually be completed through Skype in an hour or less.)

Step 1: Discuss the topic and decide on a rough thesis.

Step 2: Create 3-4 topic sentences.

Step 3: Discuss and discover evidence to support the student’s topic sentences. This step is usually completed by the student as homework.

Step 3.1 (if needed): Assist the student in the construction of one or more paragraphs. (Usually this step is reserved for students with writer’s block.)

Step 4: Read the rough draft and provide comprehensive comments and suggestions for improvement (the most important step!).

Step 5: Assist the student with the introduction, conclusion, and provide a final read-through.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Q: Is Cole really that good at personal statements?
  • A: He has enough tips, tricks, advice, and editing acumen to get his students through any number of admission essays, supplementary materials, and scholarship applications.
  • Q: How much are his tutoring services? Can you explain the pricing schedule?
  • A: He charges per hour and prices based on need. He also offers essay and college guidance packages that will cover a student through the entire application process.
  • Q: Will he help students online or through email?
  • A: Yes, but Cole believes that in-person interaction and discussion is the best way to produce flow and faciliate deep thinking. Results are not guaranteed for online help.
  • Q: How many sessions does it take to complete one 500-word essay?
  • A: It depends on many factors. Cole recommends multiple revisions (and even multiple editors). A great personal statement takes time. Many students submit a personal statement that is less than their best effort because they skip the necessary revisions and/or write their essays at the last moment.
  • Q: Will Cole write an essay for me?
  • Absolutely not. Your original words and thoughts are far more unique, interesting, and desireable than those of the tutor. ​
  • Q: When should my student begin writing his/her personal statement?
  • A: Cole recommends that the process be commenced during the summer before one's senior year.
  • Q: Does he guarantee that the student will get into [school of choice]?
  • A: No. However, Cole guarantees that you or your loved one will produce an excellent essay.
  • Q: I'm still not convinced.
  • A: The first consultation comes with homework! Lots of homework!
  • Q: Will Cole provide workshops for teachers or for large groups of students?
  • A: Yes and yes; he has strategies and scaffolds for large and small groups. Get in contact with him for more information.
